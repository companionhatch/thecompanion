Who are you really?

Patrick Close. I'm a white male settler and I work as a technical writer in Calgary, Alberta. I am also a grant writer and researcher for Inside Out Theatre.

Years ago, I graduated with a BA in English + Philosophy. I wrote about sound culture, technology, and political aesthetics. Over the past decade, I've been an editor of an arts and literary magazine, an occasional facilitator, researcher, band member, zine maker, and an one-time published poet (in a journal that no longer exists).

What's with the website?

The simplicity of this site shows my limitation as a web developer (which I am not) but also reflects three essential values about how we communicate:
(1) accessibility, (2) concentration, and (3) intention.

I believe social media platforms, which we use but do not own, sacrifice all three, and are the worst possible means to realize what is best and most valuable: human connection. For me, the reasons to go onto these platforms are the same as going to a friend's show at the most decrepit bar in town where you can't hear or see anyone and everything smells a bit ugly. Let there be better venues. But for now we dance and make do.
